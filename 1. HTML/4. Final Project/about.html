About Me

Hey there! I'm nobody in particular, a programmer, tech enthusiast, and all-around curious mind from Concordia, Entre Ríos, Argentina. I've dived deep into the world of IT, focusing on coding and robotic process automation. I graduated as a Superior Technician in Programming from UTN and as a University Technician in Web Development from UNER. Oh, and I can navigate through TypeScript, JavaScript, Python, Java—basically, if it's code, I'm on it. Also, did you know that Argentina has more psychologists per capita than any other country? Wild, right?

Beyond the screen, music is my second language. You'll probably find me jamming on my electric or acoustic guitar, tinkering on the piano, or maybe rating another album on my meticulously detailed music log. Speaking of which, Counting Crows at Woodstock '99—best live performance ever. No debate. I'm also working toward mastering the solo of 'Stricken' by Disturbed, and I'm on a mission to write a full album by the end of the year. Oh, and I listen to entire albums—none of that shuffle nonsense.

I'm also building this idea called 'GamesBrainz'—think MusicBrainz but for video games. Metadata is kind of my thing. I keep all sorts of lists: games I've beaten, albums I've heard (rated 0-100, of course), even guitars I've played. Sometimes I wonder if I should list all the lists I have. Would that be... meta? Hmm.

When I'm not lost in code or music, I'm diving into history, war stories, or the occasional mystery. Paranormal stuff too. Like, have you ever smelled something that isn't there? My grandmother's perfume sometimes lingers, and—well, that's a story for another time.

Travel is always on my mind. I'd love to explore Japan—especially its ancient temples, mountain villages, and those relaxing onsens. But my curiosity doesn't stop there. South Korea's vibrant city life, Taiwan's night markets, and China's historical landmarks are calling too. Maybe even a detour to Vietnam or Thailand for their rich history and landscapes. Until then, I'll settle for mate before and after dinner—it's the little things.

Lately, I'm on a mission to learn Japanese (N3 this year, fingers crossed) and maybe pick up another language because why not? Oh, and I'm getting back into fitness—slowly but surely. I used to walk 6 km regularly, but now I'm easing back with shorter walks and home workouts. Gotta balance all that coding and music with some movement, right?

So, that's me—kind of. A programmer, musician, history buff, list-maker, and occasional philosopher. Oh, and if you need someone to debate politics or just talk about how underrated certain anime are, I'm your person.

Hit me up if you wanna chat, or something.
